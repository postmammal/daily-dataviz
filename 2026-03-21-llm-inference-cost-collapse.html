Key Finding
The 50× cost collapse in four years is the fastest sustained pricing decline in computing history — outpacing DRAM and disk storage deflation curves. At the current trajectory of ~10× per year, frontier-grade AI inference approaches near-zero marginal cost by 2028, fundamentally changing product economics for AI-native applications.

Key Finding
The market has bifurcated sharply: a dense budget tier below $0.50/M where most teams run day-to-day workloads, and a sparse frontier tier above $3/M for the highest-stakes tasks. The "missing middle" is being hollowed out as providers race to both extremes simultaneously.
