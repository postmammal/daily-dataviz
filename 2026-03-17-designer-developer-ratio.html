1 Researcher : 5 Designers : 50 Devs
The Ratio Nobody's Hitting

Industry benchmarks say healthy product teams run at roughly 1:5:50 (researcher:designer:developer). The reality: most organizations operate at 1:20 or worse — and researchers are effectively invisible at 1 per 100 engineers.

Designers & Researchers per 100 Developers — Actual vs. Recommended
Key Finding
The design gap is stark, but the research gap is jaw-dropping. At 1 researcher per 100 developers, most teams have essentially zero dedicated research capacity. The recommended ratio is 1 per 50 — but even "well-resourced" teams rarely hit 1 per 30. Research is being done by nobody, or everybody, equally badly.

Designers per 100 Developers — by Company Stage
Key Finding
Counter-intuitively, early-stage startups often have better designer-to-developer ratios than mid-size companies — because founding designers wear many hats. The ratio deteriorates as engineering headcount scales faster than design hiring. Enterprise orgs are the worst offenders: legacy headcount structures rarely caught up with modern design practice.

Impact of Designer-to-Developer Ratio on Product Quality Metrics (%)
Key Finding
Better ratios correlate with measurable product outcomes. Teams running at 1:8 or better report significantly higher NPS scores, lower design rework rates, and fewer post-launch usability bugs. The business case for hiring more designers has always been there — it's just rarely made quantitatively.
